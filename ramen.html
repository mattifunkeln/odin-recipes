Ramen

Description

Ramen (拉麺, ラーメン or らあめん, rāmen)is a Japanese noodle dish. It includes Chinese-style wheat noodles (中華麺, chūkamen) served in a broth. Common flavors are soy sauce and miso, with typical toppings including sliced pork (chāshū), nori (dried seaweed), menma (bamboo shoots), and scallions.

The origins of ramen can be traced back to Yokohama Chinatown in the early 20th century. Today, ramen is a cultural icon in Japan, with many regional varieties and a wide range of toppings. Ramen's popularity has spread outside of Japan, and has also made its way into Western restaurant chains.
